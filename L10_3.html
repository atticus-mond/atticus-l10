Probing Questions

  1. Are our numbers truly accurate (single source of truth) or are teams keeping side spreadsheets?
  2. Which KPIs are trending downward even if still above target?
  3. Are we relying too much on lagging metrics (revenue, CSAT) and not enough on leading indicators?
  4. Where are we seeing variability or outliers we've dismissed as one-offs?
  5. Are any metrics "green" on the surface but masking hidden issues?
  6. What are clients not saying directly but hinting at in tone, requests, or behaviour?
  7. Have any small complaints been brushed off as minor that could signal a bigger issue?
  8. How is the market shifting (competition, pricing, talent expectations) and are we adapting?
  9. Are we listening to client feedback loops (CSAT, NPS, ticket trends) or just reporting scores?
  10. Are we delivering what clients need today but missing what they'll need in 6–12 months?
  11. What's the biggest client frustration we know about but haven't solved yet?
  12. Does this issue affect more than one person, team, or department?
  13. If unresolved, will it have a client-facing impact?
  14. How does this issue tie back to our 3-year picture / quarterly rocks / client success?
